3. Experiment

       The final goal in the Molecular Governor project is to establish a feedback control method for regulating a function of a biochemical reaction system. To this end, the most important device is the membrane with a desired transmission rate, and the most important technology is how to create such a membrane. In BIOMOD2014, we focus on the manufacturing of the membrane. Because we have already proposed the design theory in the “Design” section, in this section we try to validate it experimentally.

3.1 Break through

       You may consider that it would be a difficult to make such a membrane as shown in Fig. 1 even if the design theory is correct. The difficulty comes from the following matters.


● It’s difficult to make a small hole such a    nanometer scale exactly in membrane.

● We have potential to tear a membrane by using    small membrane in experiment.

At the moment, many kinds of permeable membrane are available. To create one, a technique to precisely make a small pore on a liposome sheet may be highlighted although, unfortunately, it is still a difficult work. In addition, although such a “sheet-type” permeable membrane (Fig. 1) would be a wonderful material, to handle or integrate it in an existing system would be a troublesome problem. Our breakthrough point is to provide a concept for making a permeable membrane quite easier.

Fig.1  Typical realization of permeable membrane

3.1.1 Problem


● It’s difficult to exactly make a nano-scale pore on    a sheet.

● It’s difficult to handle or integrate it in an    exisiting system.

Now, we propose the following solution. A permeable membrane is created by sticking poles, which would be made by DNA origami, on a not-permeable sheet. Each pole has many pores that provide a permeable property. In BIOMOD2014, to confirm the principle we employed a ceramic membrane filter (NGK Insulators, ltd.) as a pole in which the concept, the three-dimensional structure, and the flux flow of solute are exactly the same with our idea. Fig.3 illustrates the flux flow of solute and solvent.

Fig.2 Ceramic membrane filter (NGK Insulators, Itd.)

3.1.2 Solution


● The three-dimensional structure and the flux flow    of solute and solvent are exactly the same with    ouridea.

● A pole-style filter with many nanoscale pores.

● It is possible to integrate it into an existing    system by stick on a not- permeable plane.

3.2  Materials and Methods


3.2.1  Materials

● synthetic DNA oligonucleotide

Oligonucleotide synthesis is the chemical synthesis of relatively short fragments of nucleic acids with defined chemical sequence. In this time, we use DNA oligonucleotide has below characters.

Form: : freeze-dried product
Length : 20base
Sequence : 5’-ACCTCTCATCTATCACCACC-3'
Molecular weight : 5918.0

● Ceramic membrane

Ceramic membrane filter have been used and are highly popular in a abroad range of applications, including wastewater treatment and processing of enzymes, antibiotics, and other active pharmaceutical ingredients.

Meterial : Titania
Pore size : φ100 [nm]
Channel : 19
Length : φ15[cm]
Surface area  : φ3[mm]

● spectrophotometer

Spectrophotometer has enhanced design features enabling rapid calculations of various parameters based upon the sequence of oligonucleotide. Selection of the appropriate mode and calculation key gives the user an output of melting temperature, absorbance, concentration and molecular weight based upon the sequence of oligonucleotide.

● Matlab

Matlab is a tool for doing numerical computations with material and vectors. We need a little knowledge of any computer programming and understand concepts like variables, constants, expression and statements. We can calculate and simulate with them

3.2.2  Method

       We make a water tank such Fig. 4 to measure the concentration change of oligo DNA after it permeates membrane by using above materials and equipment (Fig. 4). Moreover, we compare the concentration change between Matlab simulation and result of experiment.

Fig.4  overall view of experiment

3.2.3 Procedure of experiment

       Before we mention about procedure, we simulate of concentration change by reference to parameter of DNA and membrane that we use in experiment.


(i) Take a solution consists of DNA and flow it in water tank.

(ii) Check the concentration change per unit time by using spectrophotometer.

(iii) Compare the concentration change between simulation result and actual measurement.

3.3 Result

       Fig.5 shows two kind of time-course from the simulation with parameter of DNA and membrane that we use in experiment and the result with parameter of actual measurement in experiment.

Fig5. Comparison of concentration change (blue, Matrab simulation; red, actual measurement)

Fig.5 illustrates that the theoretical value and actual measurement value of concentration change tend to increase until 45 second. Therefore, the result of our experiment provides evidence that DNA permeates membrane. After 45 second, the concentration of DNA shows unstable concentration change.
